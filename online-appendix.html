Online Appendix for ‘Macroeconomic Stabilization and Endogenous Inequality in a TANK Model with Dual Nominal Rigidities’

This online appendix reports the nonlinear primitives of the model and the intermediate log-linearization steps underlying Section 3 of the main text. Appendix A in the main text derives the analytical reduction from the nonredundant 20-equation system to the five-equation canonical representation.

Model Description

The appendix first states the nonlinear problems of the labor packer, households, firms, and government. It then collects a reduced nonlinear equilibrium system sufficient for the first-order approximation, and finally reports the nonredundant log-linear system used in Section 3 of the main text.

Throughout, upper-case letters denote level variables. The index \(h\in[0,1]\) refers to households, and the index \(j\in[0,1]\) refers to intermediate-goods firms. A superscript \(i\in\{H,S\}\) indicates the household type, where (\(H\)) denotes hand-to-mouth households and \(S\) denotes saver households. \(P_{t}\) denotes the final-good price index, \(W^{N}_{t}\) the aggregate nominal wage, and \(W_{t}\equiv W^{N}_{t}/P_{t}\) the aggregate real wage. For each household type \(i\), \(C^{i}_{t}\), \(N^{i}_{t}\), and \(W^{N,i}_{t}\) denote consumption, labor supply, and the nominal wage, while \(W^{i}_{t}\equiv W^{N,i}_{t}/P_{t}\) denotes the corresponding real wage. Aggregate price inflation is defined as \(\Pi^{p}_{t}\equiv P_{t}/P_{t-1}\), and type-specific wage inflation is defined as \(\Pi^{i}_{t}\equiv W^{N,i}_{t}/W^{N,i}_{t-1}\). Preferences are represented by a general period utility function \(U(C,N)\), with marginal utilities \(U_{C}(C,N)>0\) and \(U_{N}(C,N)<0\). The marginal rate of substitution is \(MRS(C,N)\equiv-U_{N}(C,N)/U_{C}(C,N)\). Intermediate firms use the general technology \(Y=A_{t}F(N)\), with \(F_{N}(N)>0\). The type-\(i\) wage markup is \(MU^{i}_{t}\equiv W^{i}_{t}/MRS(C^{i}_{t},N^{i}_{t})\), and the price markup is \(MU^{p}_{t}\equiv A_{t}F_{N}(N_{t})/W_{t}\). Finally, \(R^{N}_{t}\) denotes the gross nominal interest rate and \(D_{t}\) firm dividends. Here \(T^{i}_{t}\) denotes the net fiscal liability of type-\(i\) households. A negative value therefore corresponds to a transfer received by that household type. The variable \(Z_{t}\) denotes the transfer directed to Type H households, and \(z_{t}\) is the redistribution shock that drives \(Z_{t}\) through \(Z_{t}=z_{t}Y\).

Labor Packer

The labor packer is a competitive aggregator that combines differentiated household labor into a homogeneous labor input sold to intermediate-goods firms. Household \(h\) supplies labor \(N_{t}(h)\) and is paid the nominal wage \(W^{N}_{t}(h)\), while the packer sells the aggregate labor input \(N_{t}\) at the aggregate nominal wage \(W^{N}_{t}\). The parameter \(\psi_{w}>1\) is the elasticity of substitution across labor varieties, and \(\Lambda^{L}_{t}\) is the multiplier on the constant-elasticity-of-substitution aggregation constraint. Taking the household-specific wages \(\{W^{N}_{t}(h)\}_{h\in[0,1]}\) as given, the labor packer chooses \(\{N_{t}(h)\}_{h\in[0,1]}\) and \(N_{t}\), which yields the labor demand schedule for each household and the aggregate nominal wage index.

  • Objects taken as given: \(P_{t},W^{N}_{t},\{W^{N}_{t}(h)\}_{h\in[0,1]}\)

  • Choice variables: \(N_{t},\{N_{t}(h)\}_{h\in[0,1]}\)

  • Objective Function: \[W^{N}_{t}N_{t}-\int^{1}_{0}W^{N}_{t}(h)N_{t}(h)dh\]

  • Constraints: \[N_{t}=\left(\int^{1}_{0}N_{t}(h)^{\frac{\psi_{w}-1}{\psi_{w}}}dh\right)^{\frac{\psi_{w}}{\psi_{w}-1}},\;\psi_{w}>1.\]

  • Lagrangian: \[\begin{aligned} \mathcal{L}_{t}= & W^{N}_{t}N_{t}-\int^{1}_{0}W^{N}_{t}(h)N_{t}(h)dh\\ & +\Lambda^{L}_{t}\left(\left(\int^{1}_{0}N_{t}(h)^{\frac{\psi_{w}-1}{\psi_{w}}}dh\right)^{\frac{\psi_{w}}{\psi_{w}-1}}-N_{t}\right) \end{aligned}\]

  • FOC wrt \(N_{t}\): \[W^{N}_{t}=\Lambda^{L}_{t}\]

  • FOC wrt \(N_{t}(h)\): \[N_{t}(h)=N_{t}\left(\frac{W^{N}_{t}(h)}{W^{N}_{t}}\right)^{-\psi_{w}}\]

  • Nominal wage: \[W^{N}_{t}=\left[\int^{1}_{0}W^{N}_{t}(h)^{1-\psi_{w}}dh\right]^{\frac{1}{1-\psi_{w}}}\]

Households

There is a continuum of households indexed by \(h\in[0,1]\). Each household consumes \(C_{t}(h)\), supplies differentiated labor \(N_{t}(h)\), and sets its own nominal wage \(W^{N}_{t}(h)\). Preferences are common across households and are represented by period utility \(U(C_{t}(h),N_{t}(h))\). Wage setting is subject to Rotemberg-type adjustment costs governed by \(\eta_{w}\), and labor income is subsidized at rate \(\tau_{w}\). A fraction \(\lambda\) of households are hand-to-mouth households (Type H), who do not trade assets, while the remaining fraction \(1-\lambda\) are saver households (Type S), who hold one-period nominal bonds and receive firm dividends. Within each type, full risk sharing implies identical consumption, labor, and wage choices in equilibrium.

With two household types and within-type symmetry, the labor-packer aggregation functions defined and derived in the previous subsection can be rewritten as follows: \[\begin{aligned} N_{t} & =\left[\lambda(N^{H}_{t})^{\frac{\psi_{w}-1}{\psi_{w}}}+(1-\lambda)(N^{S}_{t})^{\frac{\psi_{w}-1}{\psi_{w}}}\right]^{\frac{\psi_{w}}{\psi_{w}-1}}\\ W^{N}_{t} & =\left[\lambda(W^{N,H}_{t})^{1-\psi_{w}}+(1-\lambda)(W^{N,S}_{t})^{1-\psi_{w}}\right]^{\frac{1}{1-\psi_{w}}}\\ & =P_{t}\left[\lambda(W^{H}_{t})^{1-\psi_{w}}+(1-\lambda)(W^{S}_{t})^{1-\psi_{w}}\right]^{\frac{1}{1-\psi_{w}}} \end{aligned}\]

Type H

A Type H household chooses current consumption \(C_{t}(h)\), labor supply \(N_{t}(h)\), and its own nominal wage \(W^{N}_{t}(h)\), taking aggregate variables as given. Since it does not trade financial assets, its current uses of funds consist of consumption and the net fiscal liability \(T^{H}_{t}\), where \(T^{H}_{t}<0\) corresponds to a transfer received from the government. In the budget constraint, \(P_{t}\) is the aggregate price level, \(Y_{t}\) is aggregate output, and \(\eta_{w}\) measures the cost of changing the nominal wage relative to the previous period’s wage \(W^{N}_{t-1}(h)\). The optimality conditions imply the marginal utility of consumption, the household-specific labor demand schedule, and the Type H wage-setting equation. In symmetric equilibrium, these conditions can be written in terms of the Type H real wage \(W^{H}_{t}\), wage inflation \(\Pi^{H}_{t}\), marginal rate of substitution \(MRS^{H}_{t}\equiv MRS(C^{H}_{t},N^{H}_{t})\), and wage markup \(MU^{H}_{t}\equiv W^{H}_{t}/MRS^{H}_{t}\).

  • Objects taken as given: \(W^{N}_{t},P_{t},N_{t},Y_{t},T^{H}_{t},W^{N}_{t-1}(h)\)

  • Choice variables: \(C_{t}(h),N_{t}(h),W^{N}_{t}(h)\)

  • Objective Function: \[\mathbb{E}_{0}\sum^{\infty}_{t=0}\beta^{t}U(C_{t}(h),N_{t}(h))\]

  • Constraints: \[\begin{aligned} & C_{t}(h)+T^{H}_{t}\\ & =(1+\tau_{w})\frac{W^{N}_{t}(h)}{P_{t}}N_{t}(h)-\frac{\eta_{w}}{2}\left(\frac{W^{N}_{t}(h)}{W^{N}_{t-1}(h)}-1\right)^{2}Y_{t}\\ & N_{t}(h)=N_{t}\left(\frac{W^{N}_{t}(h)}{W^{N}_{t}}\right)^{-\psi_{w}} \end{aligned}\]

  • Lagrangian: \[\begin{aligned} \mathbb{L}= & \mathbb{E}_{0}\sum^{\infty}_{t=0}\beta^{t}\mathcal{L}_{t}\\ \mathcal{L}_{t}= & U\left(C_{t}(h),N_{t}\left(\frac{W^{N}_{t}(h)}{W^{N}_{t}}\right)^{-\psi_{w}}\right)\\ & +\Lambda^{H}_{t}(h)\left(\begin{array}{c} (1+\tau_{w})\frac{N_{t}}{P_{t}}W^{N}_{t}\left(\frac{W^{N}_{t}(h)}{W^{N}_{t}}\right)^{1-\psi_{w}}-\frac{\eta_{w}}{2}\left(\frac{W^{N}_{t}(h)}{W^{N}_{t-1}(h)}-1\right)^{2}Y_{t}\\ -C_{t}(h)-T^{H}_{t} \end{array}\right) \end{aligned}\]

  • FOC wrt \(C_{t}(h)\): \[\Lambda^{H}_{t}(h)=U_{C}(C_{t}(h),N_{t}(h))\]

  • FOC wrt \(W^{N}_{t}(h)\): \[\begin{aligned} -\psi_{w}\frac{N_{t}}{W^{N}_{t}}\left(\frac{W^{N}_{t}(h)}{W^{N}_{t}}\right)^{-\psi_{w}-1}U_{N}(C_{t}(h),N_{t}(h))\\ +(1-\psi_{w})\Lambda^{H}_{t}(h)(1+\tau_{w})\frac{N_{t}}{P_{t}}\left(\frac{W^{N}_{t}(h)}{W^{N}_{t}}\right)^{-\psi_{w}}\\ -\Lambda^{H}_{t}(h)\eta_{w}\left(\frac{W^{N}_{t}(h)}{W^{N}_{t-1}(h)}-1\right)\frac{Y_{t}}{W^{N}_{t-1}(h)}\\ +\beta \mathbb{E}_{t}\Lambda^{H}_{t+1}(h)\eta_{w}\left(\frac{W^{N}_{t+1}(h)}{W^{N}_{t}(h)}-1\right)\frac{W^{N}_{t+1}(h)Y_{t+1}}{W^{N}_{t}(h)^{2}} & =0 \end{aligned}\]

  • Symmetric Equilibrium (\(\Lambda^{H}_{t}(h)=\Lambda^{H}_{t}\), \(C_{t}(h)=C^{H}_{t}\), \(N_{t}(h)=N^{H}_{t}\), \(W^{N}_{t}(h)=W^{N,H}_{t}\)): \[\begin{aligned} N^{H}_{t} & =N_{t}\left(\frac{W^{N,H}_{t}}{W^{N}_{t}}\right)^{-\psi_{w}}\\ \Lambda^{H}_{t} & =U_{C}(C^{H}_{t},N^{H}_{t}) \end{aligned}\] \[\begin{aligned} \eta_{w}\frac{Y_{t}}{N^{H}_{t}}\frac{W^{N,H}_{t}}{W^{N,H}_{t-1}}\left(\frac{W^{N,H}_{t}}{W^{N,H}_{t-1}}-1\right)= & \psi_{w}\left[MRS^{H}_{t}-(1+\tau_{w})\left(1-\frac{1}{\psi_{w}}\right)\frac{W^{N,H}_{t}}{P_{t}}\right]\\ & +\beta\eta_{w}\mathbb{E}_{t}\left[\frac{U_{C}(C^{H}_{t+1},N^{H}_{t+1})}{U_{C}(C^{H}_{t},N^{H}_{t})}\frac{Y_{t+1}}{N^{H}_{t}}\frac{W^{N,H}_{t+1}}{W^{N,H}_{t}}\left(\frac{W^{N,H}_{t+1}}{W^{N,H}_{t}}-1\right)\right]\\ \Leftrightarrow\eta_{w}\frac{W^{N,H}_{t}}{W^{N,H}_{t-1}}\left(\frac{W^{N,H}_{t}}{W^{N,H}_{t-1}}-1\right)= & \psi_{w}\frac{W^{N,H}_{t}N^{H}_{t}}{P_{t}Y_{t}}\left[\frac{1}{MU^{H}_{t}}-(1+\tau_{w})\left(1-\frac{1}{\psi_{w}}\right)\right]\\ & +\beta\eta_{w}\mathbb{E}_{t}\left[\frac{U_{C}(C^{H}_{t+1},N^{H}_{t+1})}{U_{C}(C^{H}_{t},N^{H}_{t})}\frac{Y_{t+1}}{Y_{t}}\frac{W^{N,H}_{t+1}}{W^{N,H}_{t}}\left(\frac{W^{N,H}_{t+1}}{W^{N,H}_{t}}-1\right)\right] \end{aligned}\]

Type S

A Type S household chooses consumption \(C_{t}(h)\), labor supply \(N_{t}(h)\), its own nominal wage \(W^{N}_{t}(h)\), and one-period nominal bond holdings \(B^{N}_{t}(h)\). In addition to current after-subsidy labor income, it receives gross nominal bond returns \(R^{N}_{t-1}B^{N}_{t-1}(h)\), firm dividends \(D_{t}\), and faces the net fiscal liability \(T^{S}_{t}\). A positive value of \(T^{S}_{t}\) is therefore a levy, while a negative value corresponds to a transfer. Here \(R^{N}_{t}\) denotes the gross nominal interest rate on the bond purchased in period \(t\), and \(B^{N}_{t}(h)/P_{t}\) is the household’s real bond position. The optimality conditions deliver both an Euler equation governing intertemporal consumption smoothing and a wage-setting equation analogous to that of Type H households. In symmetric equilibrium, these conditions are summarized by the Type S real wage \(W^{S}_{t}\), wage inflation \(\Pi^{S}_{t}\), marginal rate of substitution \(MRS^{S}_{t}\equiv MRS(C^{S}_{t},N^{S}_{t})\), and wage markup \(MU^{S}_{t}\equiv W^{S}_{t}/MRS^{S}_{t}\).

  • Objects taken as given: \(W^{N}_{t},P_{t},R^{N}_{t},D_{t},N_{t},Y_{t},T^{S}_{t},W^{N}_{t-1}(h),B^{N}_{t-1}(h)\)

  • Choice variables: \(C_{t}(h),N_{t}(h),W^{N}_{t}(h),B^{N}_{t}(h)\)

  • Objective Function: \[\mathbb{E}_{0}\sum^{\infty}_{t=0}\beta^{t}U(C_{t}(h),N_{t}(h))\]

  • Constraints: \[\begin{aligned} & C_{t}(h)+\frac{B^{N}_{t}(h)}{P_{t}}+T^{S}_{t}\\ & =(1+\tau_{w})\frac{W^{N}_{t}(h)}{P_{t}}N_{t}(h)-\frac{\eta_{w}}{2}\left(\frac{W^{N}_{t}(h)}{W^{N}_{t-1}(h)}-1\right)^{2}Y_{t}\\ & +R^{N}_{t-1}\frac{B^{N}_{t-1}(h)}{P_{t}}+D_{t}\\ & N_{t}(h)=N_{t}\left(\frac{W^{N}_{t}(h)}{W^{N}_{t}}\right)^{-\psi_{w}} \end{aligned}\]

  • Lagrangian: \[\begin{aligned} \mathbb{L}= & \mathbb{E}_{0}\sum^{\infty}_{t=0}\beta^{t}\mathcal{L}_{t}\\ \mathcal{L}_{t}= & U\left(C_{t}(h),N_{t}\left(\frac{W^{N}_{t}(h)}{W^{N}_{t}}\right)^{-\psi_{w}}\right)\\ & +\Lambda^{S}_{t}(h)\left(\begin{array}{c} (1+\tau_{w})\frac{W^{N}_{t}N_{t}}{P_{t}}\left(\frac{W^{N}_{t}(h)}{W^{N}_{t}}\right)^{1-\psi_{w}}-\frac{\eta_{w}}{2}\left(\frac{W^{N}_{t}(h)}{W^{N}_{t-1}(h)}-1\right)^{2}Y_{t}\\ +R^{N}_{t-1}\frac{B^{N}_{t-1}(h)}{P_{t}}+D_{t}-C_{t}(h)-\frac{B^{N}_{t}(h)}{P_{t}}-T^{S}_{t} \end{array}\right) \end{aligned}\]

  • FOC wrt \(C_{t}(h)\): \[\Lambda^{S}_{t}(h)=U_{C}(C_{t}(h),N_{t}(h))\]

  • FOC wrt \(W^{N}_{t}(h)\): \[\begin{aligned} -\psi_{w}\frac{N_{t}}{W^{N}_{t}}\left(\frac{W^{N}_{t}(h)}{W^{N}_{t}}\right)^{-\psi_{w}-1}U_{N}(C_{t}(h),N_{t}(h))\\ +(1-\psi_{w})\Lambda^{S}_{t}(h)(1+\tau_{w})\frac{N_{t}}{P_{t}}\left(\frac{W^{N}_{t}(h)}{W^{N}_{t}}\right)^{-\psi_{w}}\\ -\Lambda^{S}_{t}(h)\eta_{w}\left(\frac{W^{N}_{t}(h)}{W^{N}_{t-1}(h)}-1\right)\frac{Y_{t}}{W^{N}_{t-1}(h)}\\ +\beta \mathbb{E}_{t}\Lambda^{S}_{t+1}(h)\eta_{w}\left(\frac{W^{N}_{t+1}(h)}{W^{N}_{t}(h)}-1\right)\frac{W^{N}_{t+1}(h)Y_{t+1}}{W^{N}_{t}(h)^{2}} & =0 \end{aligned}\]

  • FOC wrt \(B^{N}_{t}(h)\): \[\frac{\Lambda^{S}_{t}(h)}{P_{t}}=\beta\mathbb{E}_{t}\left[R^{N}_{t}\frac{\Lambda^{S}_{t+1}(h)}{P_{t+1}}\right]\]

  • Symmetric Equilibrium (\(\Lambda^{S}_{t}(h)=\Lambda^{S}_{t}\), \(C_{t}(h)=C^{S}_{t}\), \(N_{t}(h)=N^{S}_{t}\), \(W^{N}_{t}(h)=W^{N,S}_{t}\), where \(W^{S}_{t}\) is the real wage of type \(S\)): \[\begin{aligned} N^{S}_{t} & =N_{t}\left(\frac{W^{N,S}_{t}}{W^{N}_{t}}\right)^{-\psi_{w}}\\ \Lambda^{S}_{t} & =U_{C}(C^{S}_{t},N^{S}_{t})\\ 1= & \beta\mathbb{E}_{t}\left[R^{N}_{t}\frac{U_{C}(C^{S}_{t+1},N^{S}_{t+1})}{U_{C}(C^{S}_{t},N^{S}_{t})}\frac{P_{t}}{P_{t+1}}\right] \end{aligned}\] \[\begin{aligned} \eta_{w}\frac{Y_{t}}{N^{S}_{t}}\frac{W^{N,S}_{t}}{W^{N,S}_{t-1}}\left(\frac{W^{N,S}_{t}}{W^{N,S}_{t-1}}-1\right)= & \psi_{w}\left[MRS^{S}_{t}-(1+\tau_{w})\left(1-\frac{1}{\psi_{w}}\right)\frac{W^{N,S}_{t}}{P_{t}}\right]\\ & +\beta\eta_{w}\mathbb{E}_{t}\left[\frac{U_{C}(C^{S}_{t+1},N^{S}_{t+1})}{U_{C}(C^{S}_{t},N^{S}_{t})}\frac{Y_{t+1}}{N^{S}_{t}}\frac{W^{N,S}_{t+1}}{W^{N,S}_{t}}\left(\frac{W^{N,S}_{t+1}}{W^{N,S}_{t}}-1\right)\right]\\ \Leftrightarrow\eta_{w}\frac{W^{N,S}_{t}}{W^{N,S}_{t-1}}\left(\frac{W^{N,S}_{t}}{W^{N,S}_{t-1}}-1\right)= & \psi_{w}\frac{W^{N,S}_{t}N^{S}_{t}}{P_{t}Y_{t}}\left[\frac{1}{MU^{S}_{t}}-(1+\tau_{w})\left(1-\frac{1}{\psi_{w}}\right)\right]\\ & +\beta\eta_{w}\mathbb{E}_{t}\left[\frac{U_{C}(C^{S}_{t+1},N^{S}_{t+1})}{U_{C}(C^{S}_{t},N^{S}_{t})}\frac{Y_{t+1}}{Y_{t}}\frac{W^{N,S}_{t+1}}{W^{N,S}_{t}}\left(\frac{W^{N,S}_{t+1}}{W^{N,S}_{t}}-1\right)\right] \end{aligned}\] where \(MRS^{S}_{t}=MRS(C^{S}_{t},N^{S}_{t})\) is Type S marginal rate of substitution, and \(MU^{S}_{t}=W^{S}_{t}/MRS^{S}_{t}\) is Type S wage markup.

Final Goods Firm

The final-goods firm is perfectly competitive and combines differentiated intermediate goods into a homogeneous final good using a constant-elasticity-of-substitution aggregator. The quantity of variety \(j\) is denoted by \(Y_{t}(j)\), its price by \(P_{t}(j)\), aggregate final output by \(Y_{t}\), and the final-good price index by \(P_{t}\). The elasticity of substitution across intermediate varieties is \(\psi_{p}\ge1\), and \(\Lambda^{F}_{t}\) is the multiplier on the aggregation constraint. Taking the prices \(\{P_{t}(j)\}_{j\in[0,1]}\) as given, the final-goods firm chooses (\(\{Y_{t}(j)\}_{j\in[0,1]}\)) and \(Y_{t}\), which yields the demand schedule for each intermediate-good producer and the aggregate final-good price index.

  • Objects taken as given: \(P_{t},\{P_{t}(j)\}_{j\in[0,1]}\)

  • Choice variables: \(Y_{t},\{Y_{t}(j)\}_{j\in[0,1]}\)

  • Objective Function: \[P_{t}Y_{t}-\int^{1}_{0}P_{t}(j)Y_{t}(j)dj\]

  • Constraints: \[Y_{t}=\left(\int^{1}_{0}Y_{t}(j)^{\frac{\psi_{p}-1}{\psi_{p}}}dj\right)^{\frac{\psi_{p}}{\psi_{p}-1}},\;\psi_{p}\geq1\]

  • Lagrangian: \[\begin{aligned} \mathcal{L}_{t}= & P_{t}Y_{t}-\int^{1}_{0}P_{t}(j)Y_{t}(j)dj\\ & +\Lambda^{F}_{t}\left(\left(\int^{1}_{0}Y_{t}(j)^{\frac{\psi_{p}-1}{\psi_{p}}}dj\right)^{\frac{\psi_{p}}{\psi_{p}-1}}-Y_{t}\right) \end{aligned}\]

  • FOC wrt \(Y_{t}\): \[\Lambda^{F}_{t}=P_{t}\]

  • FOC wrt \(Y_{t}(j)\): \[Y_{t}(j)=Y_{t}\left(\frac{P_{t}(j)}{P_{t}}\right)^{-\psi_{p}}\]

  • Price of Final Goods: \[P_{t}=\left[\int^{1}_{0}P_{t}(j)^{1-\psi_{p}}dj\right]^{\frac{1}{1-\psi_{p}}}\]

Intermediate Goods Firms

Intermediate-good firm \(j\) produces output \(Y_{t}(j)\) using labor \(N_{t}(j)\) according to the general technology \(Y_{t}(j)=A_{t}F(N_{t}(j))\), where \(A_{t}\equiv\exp(a_{t})\) denotes aggregate labor productivity and \(F_{N}(N)>0\). The firm hires homogeneous labor from the labor packer at the nominal wage \(W^{N}_{t}\), sets its own price \(P_{t}(j)\), and faces Rotemberg price adjustment costs governed by \(\eta_{p}\). Its real profits are denoted by \(D_{t}(j)\), the government levies the lump-sum tax \(T^{M}_{t}\), and the sales subsidy rate is \(\tau_{p}\). The firm’s objective is the date-0 present discounted value of profits, discounted by the saver household’s stochastic discount factor. The optimality conditions determine labor demand, real marginal cost, and the price-setting equation. In symmetric equilibrium, the price markup is \(MU^{p}_{t}\equiv P_{t}MPN_{t}/W^{N}_{t}=MPN_{t}/W_{t}\) and \(MPN_{t}\equiv A_{t}F_{N}(N_{t})\) is the marginal product of labor.

  • Objects taken as given: \(W^{N}_{t},P_{t},Y_{t},A_{t},T^{M}_{t},C^{S}_{t},P_{t-1}(j)\)

  • Choice variables: \(N_{t}(j),P_{t}(j),Y_{t}(j)\)

  • Objective Function: \[\mathbb{E}_{0}\sum^{\infty}_{t=0}\beta^{t}\frac{U_{C}(C^{S}_{t},N^{S}_{t})}{U_{C}(C^{S}_{0},N^{S}_{0})}D_{t}(j)\]

  • Constraints: \[\begin{aligned} D_{t}(j) & =(1+\tau_{p})\frac{P_{t}(j)}{P_{t}}Y_{t}(j)-\frac{\eta_{p}}{2}\left(\frac{P_{t}(j)}{P_{t-1}(j)}-1\right)^{2}Y_{t}-\frac{W^{N}_{t}}{P_{t}}N_{t}(j)-T^{M}_{t}\\ Y_{t}(j) & =Y_{t}\left(\frac{P_{t}(j)}{P_{t}}\right)^{-\psi_{p}}\\ Y_{t}(j) & =A_{t}F(N_{t}(j)) \end{aligned}\]

  • Lagrangian: \[\begin{aligned} \mathbb{L}= & \mathbb{E}_{0}\sum^{\infty}_{t=0}\beta^{t}\frac{U_{C}(C^{S}_{t},N^{S}_{t})}{U_{C}(C^{S}_{0},N^{S}_{0})}\mathcal{L}_{t}\\ \mathcal{L}_{t}= & (1+\tau_{p})Y_{t}\left(\frac{P_{t}(j)}{P_{t}}\right)^{1-\psi_{p}}-\frac{\eta_{p}}{2}\left(\frac{P_{t}(j)}{P_{t-1}(j)}-1\right)^{2}Y_{t}-\frac{W^{N}_{t}}{P_{t}}N_{t}(j)\\ & -T^{M}_{t}+\Lambda^{M}_{t}(j)\left(A_{t}F(N_{t}(j))-Y_{t}\left(\frac{P_{t}(j)}{P_{t}}\right)^{-\psi_{p}}\right) \end{aligned}\]

  • FOC wrt \(N_{t}(j)\): \[\begin{aligned} \Lambda^{M}_{t}(j)A_{t}F_{N}(N_{t}(j)) & =W^{N}_{t}/P_{t} \end{aligned}\]

  • FOC wrt \(P_{t}(j)\): \[\begin{aligned} & U_{C}(C^{S}_{t},N^{S}_{t})(1+\tau_{p})(1-\psi_{p})\frac{Y_{t}}{P_{t}}\left(\frac{P_{t}(j)}{P_{t}}\right)^{-\psi_{p}}-U_{C}(C^{S}_{t},N^{S}_{t})\eta_{p}\frac{Y_{t}}{P_{t-1}(j)}\left(\frac{P_{t}(j)}{P_{t-1}(j)}-1\right)\\ & +U_{C}(C^{S}_{t},N^{S}_{t})\psi_{p}\Lambda^{M}_{t}(j)\frac{Y_{t}}{P_{t}}\left(\frac{P_{t}(j)}{P_{t}}\right)^{-\psi_{p}-1}+\beta\eta_{p}\mathbb{E}_{t}\left[U_{C}(C^{S}_{t+1},N^{S}_{t+1})Y_{t+1}\frac{P_{t+1}(j)}{P_{t}(j)^{2}}\left(\frac{P_{t+1}(j)}{P_{t}(j)}-1\right)\right]=0 \end{aligned}\]

  • Symmetric Equilibrium (\(N_{t}(j)=N_{t}\), \(\Lambda^{M}_{t}(j)=\Lambda^{M}_{t}\), \(P_{t}(j)=P_{t}\), \(Y_{t}(j)=Y_{t}\)): \[\begin{aligned} \eta_{p}\frac{P_{t}}{P_{t-1}}\left(\frac{P_{t}}{P_{t-1}}-1\right)= & \psi_{p}\left[\frac{W^{N}_{t}/P_{t}}{MPN_{t}}-(1+\tau_{p})\left(1-\frac{1}{\psi_{p}}\right)\right]\\ & +\beta\eta_{p}\mathbb{E}_{t}\left[\frac{U_{C}(C^{S}_{t+1},N^{S}_{t+1})}{U_{C}(C^{S}_{t},N^{S}_{t})}\frac{Y_{t+1}}{Y_{t}}\frac{P_{t+1}}{P_{t}}\left(\frac{P_{t+1}}{P_{t}}-1\right)\right]\\ = & \psi_{p}\left[\frac{1}{MU^{p}_{t}}-(1+\tau_{p})\left(1-\frac{1}{\psi_{p}}\right)\right]\\ & +\beta\eta_{p}\mathbb{E}_{t}\left[\frac{U_{C}(C^{S}_{t+1},N^{S}_{t+1})}{U_{C}(C^{S}_{t},N^{S}_{t})}\frac{Y_{t+1}}{Y_{t}}\frac{P_{t+1}}{P_{t}}\left(\frac{P_{t+1}}{P_{t}}-1\right)\right] \end{aligned}\]

  • Profits: \[\begin{aligned} D_{t} & =(1+\tau_{p})Y_{t}-\frac{\eta_{p}}{2}\left(\frac{P_{t}}{P_{t-1}}-1\right)^{2}Y_{t}-\frac{W^{N}_{t}}{P_{t}}N_{t}-T^{M}_{t} \end{aligned}\] When \((1+\tau_{p})(1-1/\psi_{p})=1\), steady state price markup is one.

Government

The government performs three functions in the model. First, it provides the wage subsidy \(\tau_{w}\) and the sales subsidy \(\tau_{p}\), eliminating steady-state markups under the baseline specialization introduced below. Second, it redistributes resources across household types through the lump-sum components \(T^{H}_{t}\) and \(T^{S}_{t}\). Here \(Z_{t}\) denotes the transfer received by Type H households, while Type S households finance that transfer through a balanced-budget levy. Because \(T^{i}_{t}\) denotes net fiscal liability, the transfer implies \(T^{H}_{t}<0\) for the recipient household type and a positive levy on Type S through \(T^{S}_{t}\). Third, the monetary authority sets the gross nominal interest rate \(R^{N}_{t}\) according to a Taylor rule that responds to gross price inflation \(\Pi^{p}_{t}\) and to the monetary policy shock \(m_{t}\). The redistribution shock \(z_{t}\) follows an autoregressive process and determines \(Z_{t}\) through \(Z_{t}=z_{t}Y\), where \(Y\) is steady-state output.

Fiscal policy: \[\begin{aligned} T^{H}_{t} & =-Z_{t}+\tau_{w}W^{H}_{t}N^{H}_{t}\\ T^{S}_{t} & =\frac{\lambda}{1-\lambda}Z_{t}+\tau_{w}W^{S}_{t}N^{S}_{t}\\ T^{M}_{t} & =\tau_{p}Y_{t}\\ \tau_{w} & =1/(\psi_{w}-1)\\ \tau_{p} & =1/(\psi_{p}-1)\\ Z_{t} & =z_{t}Y\\ z_{t} & =\rho_{z}z_{t-1}+e^{z}_{t} \end{aligned}\]

Monetary Policy:

\[\begin{aligned} R^{N}_{t} & =(1/\beta)\exp(-m_{t})(\Pi^{p}_{t})^{\phi}\\ m_{t} & =\rho_{m}m_{t-1}+e^{m}_{t} \end{aligned}\]

Equilibrium conditions

Equilibrium requires market clearing in goods and bond markets, together with consistency between aggregate variables and type-specific allocations. Bond-market clearing requires zero net bond supply, so aggregate bond holdings satisfy \(B^{N}_{t}=0\). Aggregate consumption \(C_{t}\) is the population-weighted average of \(C^{H}_{t}\) and \(C^{S}_{t}\), and final output \(Y_{t}\) is allocated to private consumption and to price and wage adjustment costs.

Final goods market: \[\begin{aligned} Y_{t} & =C_{t}+\frac{\eta_{p}}{2}\left(\frac{P_{t}}{P_{t-1}}-1\right)^{2}Y_{t}\\ & +\lambda\frac{\eta_{w}}{2}\left(\frac{W^{N,H}_{t}}{W^{N,H}_{t-1}}-1\right)^{2}Y_{t}+(1-\lambda)\frac{\eta_{w}}{2}\left(\frac{W^{N,S}_{t}}{W^{N,S}_{t-1}}-1\right)^{2}Y_{t} \end{aligned}\]

Bond market: \(B^{N}_{t}=0\).

Aggregation condition: \[C_{t}=\lambda C^{H}_{t}+(1-\lambda)C^{S}_{t}\]

Reduced nonlinear equilibrium system

Before summarizing the model, I collect the key real variables and inflation rates used below. In particular, \(W_{t}\equiv W^{N}_{t}/P_{t}\) denotes the aggregate real wage, \(W^{H}_{t}\equiv W^{N,H}_{t}/P_{t}\) and \(W^{S}_{t}\equiv W^{N,S}_{t}/P_{t}\) denote type-specific real wages, \(\Pi^{p}_{t}\equiv P_{t}/P_{t-1}\) denotes gross price inflation, and \(\Pi^{H}_{t}\), \(\Pi^{S}_{t}\), and \(\Pi^{w}_{t}\) denote the corresponding gross wage inflation rates for Type H, Type S, and the aggregate wage index. The aggregate wage markup is defined as \(MU^{w}_{t}\equiv W_{t}/MRS(C_{t},N_{t})\).

The following block collects a reduced set of nonlinear equilibrium conditions that is sufficient for the log-linearization below.

I report only the nonlinear conditions needed to obtain the first-order system below. Conditions omitted here are either redundant or recoverable by symmetry and aggregation.

The nonlinear equilibrium conditions can be summarized as follows:

  • Labor Packer: \[\begin{aligned} W_{t} & =\left[\lambda(W^{H}_{t})^{1-\psi_{w}}+(1-\lambda)(W^{S}_{t})^{1-\psi_{w}}\right]^{\frac{1}{1-\psi_{w}}}\\ N_{t} & =\left[\lambda(N^{H}_{t})^{\frac{\psi_{w}-1}{\psi_{w}}}+(1-\lambda)(N^{S}_{t})^{\frac{\psi_{w}-1}{\psi_{w}}}\right]^{\frac{\psi_{w}}{\psi_{w}-1}} \end{aligned}\]

  • Type H: \[\begin{aligned} N^{H}_{t}= & N_{t}\left(\frac{W^{H}_{t}}{W_{t}}\right)^{-\psi_{w}}\\ C^{H}_{t}= & W^{H}_{t}N^{H}_{t}+Z_{t}-\frac{\eta_{w}}{2}\left(\Pi^{H}_{t}-1\right)^{2}Y_{t}\\ \eta_{w}\Pi^{H}_{t}\left(\Pi^{H}_{t}-1\right)= & \psi_{w}\left\{ \frac{1}{MU^{H}_{t}}-1\right\} \frac{W^{H}_{t}N^{H}_{t}}{Y_{t}}\\ & +\eta_{w}\beta\mathbb{E}_{t}\left[\frac{U_{C}(C^{H}_{t+1},N^{H}_{t+1})}{U_{C}(C^{H}_{t},N^{H}_{t})}\frac{Y_{t+1}}{Y_{t}}\Pi^{H}_{t+1}\left(\Pi^{H}_{t+1}-1\right)\right]\\ \Pi^{H}_{t}= & \frac{W^{H}_{t}}{W^{H}_{t-1}}\Pi^{p}_{t}\\ MU^{H}_{t}= & \frac{W^{H}_{t}}{MRS(C^{H}_{t},N^{H}_{t})} \end{aligned}\]

  • Type S: \[\begin{aligned} N^{S}_{t}= & N_{t}\left(\frac{W^{S}_{t}}{W_{t}}\right)^{-\psi_{w}}\\ 1= & \beta\mathbb{E}_{t}\left[\frac{R^{N}_{t}}{\Pi^{p}_{t+1}}\frac{U_{C}(C^{S}_{t+1},N^{S}_{t+1})}{U_{C}(C^{S}_{t},N^{S}_{t})}\right]\\ \eta_{w}\Pi^{S}_{t}\left(\Pi^{S}_{t}-1\right)= & \psi_{w}\left\{ \frac{1}{MU^{S}_{t}}-1\right\} \frac{W^{S}_{t}N^{S}_{t}}{Y_{t}}\\ & +\eta_{w}\beta\mathbb{E}_{t}\left[\frac{U_{C}(C^{S}_{t+1},N^{S}_{t+1})}{U_{C}(C^{S}_{t},N^{S}_{t})}\frac{Y_{t+1}}{Y_{t}}\Pi^{S}_{t+1}\left(\Pi^{S}_{t+1}-1\right)\right]\\ \Pi^{S}_{t}= & \frac{W^{S}_{t}}{W^{S}_{t-1}}\Pi^{p}_{t}\\ MU^{S}_{t}= & \frac{W^{S}_{t}}{MRS(C^{S}_{t},N^{S}_{t})} \end{aligned}\]

  • Firms: \[\begin{aligned} A_{t} & =\exp(a_{t})\\ Y_{t} & =A_{t}F(N_{t})\\ a_{t} & =\rho_{a}a_{t-1}+e^{a}_{t}\\ \eta_{p}\Pi^{p}_{t}\left(\Pi^{p}_{t}-1\right) & =\psi_{p}\left\{ \frac{1}{MU^{p}_{t}}-1\right\} \\ & +\eta_{p}\beta \mathbb{E}_{t}\left[\frac{U_{C}(C^{S}_{t+1},N^{S}_{t+1})}{U_{C}(C^{S}_{t},N^{S}_{t})}\frac{Y_{t+1}}{Y_{t}}\Pi^{p}_{t+1}\left(\Pi^{p}_{t+1}-1\right)\right]\\ MU^{p}_{t} & =\frac{A_{t}F_{N}(N_{t})}{W_{t}}\\ D_{t} & =Y_{t}-\frac{\eta_{p}}{2}\left(\Pi^{p}_{t}-1\right)^{2}Y_{t}-W_{t}N_{t} \end{aligned}\]

  • Government: \[\begin{aligned} R^{N}_{t} & =\frac{\exp(-m_{t})}{\beta}(\Pi^{p}_{t})^{\phi}\\ m_{t} & =\rho_{m}m_{t-1}+e^{m}_{t}\\ Z_{t} & =z_{t}Y\\ z_{t} & =\rho_{z}z_{t-1}+e^{z}_{t} \end{aligned}\]

  • Market clearing and others:

\[\begin{aligned} Y_{t}= & C_{t}+\frac{\eta_{p}}{2}(\Pi^{p}_{t}-1)^{2}Y_{t}\\ & +\lambda\frac{\eta_{w}}{2}(\Pi^{H}_{t}-1)^{2}Y_{t}+(1-\lambda)\frac{\eta_{w}}{2}(\Pi^{S}_{t}-1)^{2}Y_{t}\\ C_{t}= & \lambda C^{H}_{t}+(1-\lambda)C^{S}_{t}\\ R_{t}= & \mathbb{E}_{t}\left[\frac{R^{N}_{t}}{\Pi^{p}_{t+1}}\right]\\ \Pi^{w}_{t}= & \frac{W_{t}}{W_{t-1}}\Pi^{p}_{t}\\ MU^{w}_{t}= & \frac{W_{t}}{MRS(C_{t},N_{t})} \end{aligned}\]

Functional specialization and log-linear approximation

From this section onward, I specialize the primitives to the separable CRRA-Frisch utility function and the linear production function: \[\begin{aligned} U(C,N) & =\frac{C^{1-\gamma}}{1-\gamma}-\frac{N^{1+\varphi}}{1+\varphi},\\ MRS(C,N) & =C^{\gamma}N^{\varphi},\\ F(N) & =N. \end{aligned}\]

In the log-linearized system, lower-case letters denote log deviations from the symmetric zero-inflation steady state; for example, \(c_{t}\equiv\log(C_{t}/C)\), \(n_{t}\equiv\log(N_{t}/N)\), and \(w_{t}\equiv\log(W_{t}/W)\). Inflation variables are log deviations of gross inflation rates, and profits are scaled by steady-state output, \(d_{t}\equiv D_{t}/Y\). The ex ante real interest rate is \(r_{t}\equiv r^{N}_{t}-\mathbb{E}_{t}\pi^{p}_{t+1}\), where \(r^{N}_{t}\) is the log deviation of the gross nominal rate \(R^{N}_{t}\). The variables \(a_{t}\), \(m_{t}\), and \(z_{t}\) are exogenous deviation processes. In what follows, upper-case \(MU\) denotes level markups, whereas lower-case \(\mu\) denotes their log-linear deviations. Around the symmetric zero-inflation steady state, the quadratic price- and wage-adjustment costs are second-order terms and therefore drop out from the first-order resource constraint, which implies \(y_{t}=c_{t}\) at first order.

The steady-state values are as follows: \[\begin{aligned} N= & N^{S}=N^{H}=1\\ Y=C= & C^{S}=C^{H}=1\\ MU^{w}= & MU^{S}=MU^{H}=1\\ \Pi^{w}= & \Pi^{S}=\Pi^{H}=\Pi^{p}=1\\ W= & W^{S}=W^{H}=1\\ D= & 0 \end{aligned}\]

The log-linear approximation, with \(d_{t}:=D_{t}/Y\), is then given by:

  • Labor Packer: \[\begin{aligned} w_{t} & =\lambda w^{H}_{t}+(1-\lambda)w^{S}_{t}\\ n_{t} & =\lambda n^{H}_{t}+(1-\lambda)n^{S}_{t} \end{aligned}\]

  • Type H: \[\begin{aligned} n^{H}_{t} & =n_{t}-\psi_{w}(w^{H}_{t}-w_{t})\\ c^{H}_{t} & =w^{H}_{t}+n^{H}_{t}+z_{t}\\ \pi^{H}_{t} & =-(\psi_{w}/\eta_{w})\mu^{H}_{t}+\beta\mathbb{E}_{t}\pi^{H}_{t+1}\\ \pi^{H}_{t} & =w^{H}_{t}-w^{H}_{t-1}+\pi^{p}_{t}\\ \mu^{H}_{t} & =w^{H}_{t}-(\gamma c^{H}_{t}+\varphi n^{H}_{t})\\ \end{aligned}\]

  • Type S: \[\begin{aligned} n^{S}_{t} & =n_{t}-\psi_{w}(w^{S}_{t}-w_{t})\\ r^{N}_{t}-\mathbb{E}_{t}\pi^{p}_{t+1} & =\gamma\mathbb{E}_{t}\Delta c^{S}_{t+1}\\ \pi^{S}_{t} & =-(\psi_{w}/\eta_{w})\mu^{S}_{t}+\beta\mathbb{E}_{t}\pi^{S}_{t+1}\\ \pi^{S}_{t} & =w^{S}_{t}-w^{S}_{t-1}+\pi^{p}_{t}\\ \mu^{S}_{t} & =w^{S}_{t}-(\gamma c^{S}_{t}+\varphi n^{S}_{t}) \end{aligned}\]

  • Firms: \[\begin{aligned} y_{t} & =a_{t}+n_{t}\\ a_{t} & =\rho_{a}a_{t-1}+e^{a}_{t}\\ \pi^{p}_{t} & =-(\psi_{p}/\eta_{p})\mu^{p}_{t}+\beta\mathbb{E}_{t}\pi^{p}_{t+1}\\ \mu^{p}_{t} & =a_{t}-w_{t}\\ d_{t} & =a_{t}-w_{t} \end{aligned}\]

  • Government: \[\begin{aligned} r^{N}_{t} & =\phi\pi^{p}_{t}-m_{t}\\ m_{t} & =\rho_{m}m_{t-1}+e^{m}_{t}\\ z_{t} & =\rho_{z}z_{t-1}+e^{z}_{t} \end{aligned}\]

  • Market clearing and others:

\[\begin{aligned} y_{t} & =c_{t}\\ r_{t} & =r^{N}_{t}-\mathbb{E}_{t}\pi^{p}_{t+1}\\ c_{t} & =\lambda c^{H}_{t}+(1-\lambda)c^{S}_{t}\\ \pi^{w}_{t} & =\lambda\pi^{H}_{t}+(1-\lambda)\pi^{S}_{t}\\ \pi^{w}_{t} & =w_{t}-w_{t-1}+\pi^{p}_{t}\\ \pi^{w}_{t} & =-(\psi_{w}/\eta_{w})\mu^{w}_{t}+\beta\mathbb{E}_{t}\pi^{w}_{t+1}\\ \mu^{w}_{t} & =w_{t}-(\gamma c_{t}+\varphi n_{t}) \end{aligned}\]

After eliminating redundant equations and the nominal rate \(r^{N}_{t}\), Table 1 reports the nonredundant 20-equation representation used in the main text. The system is written in nonredundant form. Some type-specific relations are not reported separately because they are recoverable from symmetry, aggregation, and the displayed household block.
